Basic Rules (Quick Guide)

Goal

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards.

Setup

Each player starts with 7 cards. Flip one card to start the discard pile.

On Your Turn

Play a card that matches the discard pile by color or number/symbol. If you cannot play, draw a card.

Action Cards

Special cards change the flow: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four.

Saying “UNO”

When you have one card left, you must say “UNO”. (In a real game, failing to do so can cause a penalty.)

Winning

The first player to play their last card wins the round.

Card Explanations (Flip Cards)

Hover (or focus with Tab) to flip each card and read what it does.

Number 7 Match color or number

Number Cards

Play a number card if it matches the color or the number on the discard pile.

  • Most common way to reduce your hand
  • Great for controlling colors
Skip ⦸ Next player loses a turn

Skip

When played, the next player is skipped and does not take a turn.

  • Use to stop someone close to winning
  • Use to keep the turn order favorable
Reverse ⇄ Switch direction

Reverse

Changes the direction of play. In 2-player games, this can act like a skip.

  • Great for timing when a strong opponent is next
  • Combine with color control to keep momentum
Draw 2 +2 Next player draws 2

Draw Two

The next player must draw two cards and loses their turn.

  • Use to slow down a player with a small hand
  • Good for forcing opponents to pick up cards
Wild W Choose any color

Wild

Lets you choose the next color to be played, regardless of the discard pile.

  • Best saved for when you are stuck
  • Choose a color you have the most of
Wild +4 +4 Choose color + opponent draws 4

Wild Draw Four

Choose the next color AND the next player draws four cards and loses their turn.

  • Strongest swing card — use at key moments
  • Use to block an opponent from winning

Strategy Tips

Control the Color

Try to keep a majority color in your hand so you can steer the game.

Save Wilds for Trouble

Wild cards are best when you have no other playable option.

Watch Opponents’ Hands

If someone is low on cards, use Skip/Draw cards to slow them down.

Reverse for Positioning

Reverse can change who gets the next turn — use it to dodge strong players.

Don’t Be Predictable

Mix up colors and action cards so opponents can’t anticipate your moves.

Plan Your “UNO” Turn

Try to reach one card with a strong last-play option ready.
